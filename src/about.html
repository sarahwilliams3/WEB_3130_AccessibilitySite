About Me

When I Hear "Accessibility"

I feel that accessibility is more important than ever. Without accessibility we are asking for people to leave our companyies, our blogs, or apps. When people come to our sites we want their experiences to be memorble in a way where they share and want others they know about the ease of using the page. This includes people with disabilities. More than 10% of people in the US have a vision or hearing disablily. If the internet is not accessible than we lose more 10% of people who are customers, family, or friends. This is morally wrong, but it is also a disconnection of people which is huge in families, businesses, and many other relationships.

Automated Tools are NOT Enough

If we only test with automated tools we lose the opportunity to see how our sites and functions work in reality. Just because our images have alternative text doesn't mean that the text is actually helpful to someone who uses a screen reader. We may also have something like an alert that if it pulls someone from their task can make the site hard to use and not accessible even though automated tools say that the site is.

Accessibility standards I Use

I can use ARIA labels to help those with screen readers. I can also write appropriate alternative text for images. I can make text and background color have color contrast that is readible to all people including those with visual disabilities.

Why Inclusion During Design Matters

I would include all poeple in testing and design processes because this helps the site be easier to use, not only to those with a disability, but to all people. Having transcripts on videos help those with hearing disabilities, but it also helps people who can't use their sound because of their current location. So using all poepl for testing and design allows me to make better functioning webites for all people.

My Accessibility Statement

This is an accessibility statement from Sarah Williams Foster.

Confromance Status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. Sarah's Accessibility Site is fully conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Fully conformant means that the content fully conforms to the accessibility standard without any exceptions.
